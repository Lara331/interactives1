LA Fires Ravage the Palisades: Expected to be Most Destructive in US History

By Lara Weissmann

Wildfires broke out across southern California last Tuesday. Residents brace for continued disaster as firefighters attempt to put out the flames of at least five sprawling fires at the height of the disaster, according to an ABC News Report updated on Thursday morning.

President Joe Biden declared a federal major disaster for LA County on Wednesday.

Source: NBC News

The Washington Post reported at least 24 had been killed by the flames as of Tuesday morning, seven days after the fires initially broke out. Over 12,000 structures have been destroyed, and the cause of the fires is still unknown.

Evacuation orders remain in place across LA County. There are four active fires still burning, and they include:

  • Palisades Fire (17% contained)
  • Eaton Fire (35% contained)
  • Hurst Fire (97% contained)
  • Auto Fire (0% contained)
